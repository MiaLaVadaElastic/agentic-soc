Definition

The agentic security operations platform, defined

Definition — Agentic Security Operations Platform

An agentic security operations platform is a unified system in which autonomous AI agents handle the full security lifecycle — from data ingestion and alert correlation through threat investigation and response execution — while human analysts retain final judgment, approval authority, and strategic direction. It is distinguished from legacy SIEM, XDR, and SOAR tools by its capacity for adaptive reasoning rather than rigid, pre-scripted playbooks.

The term "agentic" is precise. In AI systems design, an agent is a system that pursues a goal autonomously — choosing which tools to use, in what order, to achieve an outcome. Unlike a chatbot that responds to prompts, or a SOAR that executes pre-mapped playbooks, an AI agent reasons. It can handle attacks it has never seen before.

The critical distinction: an agentic security operations platform does not replace the human analyst. It repositions them. The platform investigates, correlates, and stages the response. The analyst reads the case, validates the reasoning, and approves the action. This is the difference between a human in the loop and a human on the loop.

No responsible security organization should deploy a fully autonomous SOC without human oversight. The agentic model is designed specifically to preserve that oversight while operating at the speed the threat environment now demands.

Not this

A fully autonomous SOC

Every significant response action requires analyst approval before execution. Removing that gate is not the agentic model — it is an unacceptable risk posture.

Not this

A SOAR with an AI layer

Legacy SOAR tools execute deterministic playbooks. Agentic platforms reason through novel situations dynamically — they are architecturally different, not incrementally better.

Not this

A next-generation SIEM

A SIEM ingests and correlates. An agentic platform ingests, reasons, investigates, builds a case, proposes a response, and executes it — in one unified system.

Core capabilities

What an agentic platform requires

Three integrated capability areas define an agentic security operations platform. These are not separate products — they are a single, unified system. Every boundary between them is a delay the adversary exploits.

01
Ingestion at scale
Perceive everything, miss nothing

An agentic platform can only reason over data it can see. Coverage gaps — whether forced by per-device pricing or rigid data pipelines — become attack surface. AI-driven threats are engineered to find unmonitored assets.

  • Universal ingestion: SIEM, endpoint, cloud, SaaS, and threat intelligence in one platform
  • Automatic schema mapping — no pipeline engineering required per new data source
  • Federated search across cloud, on-prem, and edge without forcing data centralization
  • Years of history queryable in real time — no rehydration delays during active incidents
02
Reason at machine speed
AI grounded in your data, transparent by design

The reasoning layer is where agentic platforms differ most from legacy tools. AI decisions must be grounded in your actual security data — not an abstraction layer — and transparent enough that analysts can validate every conclusion.

  • AI reasoning over your data where it lives — no connector abstraction, no data copy
  • Composable AI skills: discrete, executable capabilities agents invoke dynamically
  • Full AI transparency — visible prompts, auditable decisions, no black boxes
  • Model agnosticism — any LLM, including on-premises models for air-gapped environments
03
Prevention and response
From detection to containment at machine speed

Prevention is the fastest possible response. But when an active incident requires containment, the response must execute faster than a human queue can process. Agentic workflows — validated by a human before execution — close this gap.

  • Native automation in the same platform as detection — no separate SOAR integration
  • Adaptive reasoning over pre-scripted playbooks that break against novel attacks
  • Human approval gates before any significant action executes
  • World-class endpoint prevention with no per-device pricing that creates coverage gaps

Evaluation guide

Questions to ask before you commit to a platform

These questions are designed for CISOs and security leaders assessing platform readiness — not vendor pitches. A platform that cannot satisfy the critical criteria below cannot credibly claim the agentic label.

1

Can your platform ingest all data sources without pricing-forced coverage gaps?

Per-device or per-source pricing that requires you to choose which assets to monitor is incompatible with an agentic model. AI-driven attacks probe everything. If your coverage has gaps, your defense does too.

Critical
2

Does your platform reason adaptively, or execute pre-scripted playbooks?

Deterministic automation breaks when attackers deviate from expected patterns. An agentic platform uses skills invoked dynamically by an agent reasoning through each incident — including attacks it has never encountered before.

Critical
3

Can your analysts see and validate every AI decision?

Transparent AI — visible prompts, auditable reasoning, verifiable outputs — is not optional in a security context. If your AI cannot show its work, your analysts cannot trust its conclusions. Unauditable AI decisions are also an emerging compliance liability.

Critical
4

Is automation native to the platform, or a separate integration layer?

A standalone SOAR creates an integration point that fails during active incidents — exactly when response speed matters most. Native automation in the same system as detection eliminates this failure mode.

Important
5

Can you access historical data in real time during an active incident?

Rehydration delays that make historical logs unavailable for hours are a structural vulnerability. An agentic platform requires years of telemetry accessible at query time, with no penalty for looking back during an active campaign.

Important
6

Does the platform support model sovereignty and deployment flexibility?

Regulated organizations — particularly government and financial services — require the ability to choose their AI model, including fully disconnected on-premises models. Vendor lock-in to a single LLM is incompatible with data residency and sovereignty requirements.

Strategic
7

Is the platform open and auditable by design?

Open detection rules, open schemas, open agentic frameworks are the foundation for trust and community validation. A platform that locks its logic in proprietary formats cannot be independently verified — and cannot be adapted to your specific environment.
